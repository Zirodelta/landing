Phemex ETH/USDT Funding Rate

Phemex's ETH/USDT perpetual contract follows an 8-hour funding rate cycle at 00:00, 08:00, and 16:00 UTC. The Singapore-based derivatives exchange applies funding rates based on the premium between perpetual and mark prices, with rates typically ranging from -0.375% to +0.375% per funding period.

How Funding Works on Phemex

Phemex calculates funding rates using a time-weighted average premium over the 8-hour period, sampled every minute during the final hour before settlement. Long positions pay funding to shorts when rates are positive, indicating perpetual trading above spot price. The exchange's funding rate formula incorporates both premium and interest rate components, with the latter typically set at 0.01% per 8-hour period. Settlement occurs precisely at funding times, with positions automatically debited or credited based on notional size.

Why ETH/USDT?

The pair's consistent volume and Phemex's competitive 0.01% maker fees create favorable conditions for delta-neutral positions. Historical Funding Rate Patterns

The contract typically experiences negative funding during sharp market corrections, reaching -0.15% during the May 2024 deleveraging event. Weekend funding rates tend to be more volatile due to reduced institutional activity, with Friday evening UTC often showing elevated positive rates. Cross-referencing with traditional market hours reveals higher funding rates during US trading sessions, particularly around options expiry dates.

Phemex's 8-hour funding cycle creates systematic arbitrage opportunities when rates diverge significantly from competing exchanges like Binance or Bybit. Risk Considerations

Phemex's smaller user base compared to tier-1 exchanges creates occasional liquidity gaps, particularly during high volatility periods when funding rates are most attractive. The exchange's automatic deleveraging system can force position closures during extreme market stress, disrupting long-term funding strategies. Regulatory uncertainty in Singapore and potential restrictions on retail leverage could impact contract specifications without advance notice. Cross-exchange arbitrage faces settlement risk, as Phemex's 8-hour cycle may not align perfectly with competitors, creating basis risk between funding collection and hedging execution.
