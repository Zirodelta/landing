Bybit vs Paradex

Side-by-side comparison of funding rate mechanics, fees, and perpetual trading on Bybit and Paradex.

Verdict

Bybit offers established infrastructure and deeper liquidity for traditional funding rate arbitrage, making it suitable for larger positions and longer holding periods. Paradex provides innovative self-custodial perpetuals with frequent settlements, appealing to traders seeking non-custodial exposure despite limited market coverage.

Rate Mechanics

Bybit uses traditional 8-hour funding intervals with TWAP-based rate calculations, allowing positions to build larger imbalances before settlement. Paradex implements hourly funding on StarkNet, providing more frequent rebalancing but requiring constant monitoring. Bybit's longer intervals suit swing arbitrage strategies, while Paradex's hourly settlements favor scalping approaches. Both exchanges use similar premium-index methodologies but Paradex benefits from lower gas costs on L2.
