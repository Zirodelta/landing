Bybit vs Aevo

Side-by-side comparison of funding rate mechanics, fees, and perpetual trading on Bybit and Aevo.

Verdict

Bybit offers more predictable funding patterns with deeper liquidity pools, making it suitable for larger arbitrage positions. Aevo provides more frequent funding opportunities with transparent on-chain settlement, appealing to sophisticated traders comfortable with rollup-based infrastructure.

Rate Mechanics

Bybit uses traditional 8-hour funding cycles with premium/discount calculations based on mark price deviations from spot. Aevo implements 1-hour funding intervals on its custom rollup, allowing for more frequent rate adjustments and potentially better capital efficiency. Bybit's rates tend to be more stable due to deeper liquidity, while Aevo's hourly settlements can create more arbitrage opportunities but with higher execution complexity. Both exchanges use similar interest rate components, but Aevo's DEX structure means funding flows are transparent on-chain.
