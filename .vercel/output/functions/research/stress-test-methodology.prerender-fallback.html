Stress-Testing with Basis Risk: The Real Number

How we model spot-perp basis divergence, execution timing slippage, and funding rate noise to produce a number we trust.

Key Finding

Basis risk modeling reduces CAGR from 25.7% (theoretical) to 19.55% (stress-tested) and Sharpe from 7.57 to 3.74 — but the strategy remains strongly profitable.

basis-riskstress-testmethodologysharpe

The Problem with Naive Backtests

Most funding rate backtests assume:

  1. You enter and exit at exact settlement prices
  2. Spot and perp prices move in lockstep
  3. The trailing average rate is what you actually collect

None of these are true.

Our Three-Layer Risk Model

Layer 1: Spot-Perp Basis Divergence

Spot and perp prices can temporarily diverge. This creates mark-to-market P&L even though your position is delta-neutral by construction.

Model: Daily random P&L drawn from a normal distribution, with volatility tiered by open interest:

OI TierDaily Vol (Stress)Example
Large cap (>$50M OI)0.20%BTC, ETH
Mid cap ($5-50M OI)0.40%DOGE, AVAX
Small cap (<$5M OI)0.80%Micro-cap alts

The divergence is mean-reverting with a 10% daily pull toward zero. On exit, 50% of accumulated basis remains as realized P&L.

This is conservative — real basis divergence is typically smaller and more mean-reverting than our model.

Layer 2: Execution Timing Slippage

You can't enter at the exact settlement price. By the time your orders fill, the price has moved.

Model: 5 basis points additional cost per entry, applied to both legs. This is on top of the explicit trading fees.

Layer 3: Funding Rate Noise

The trailing average rate is not what you collect on any given settlement. Individual settlements can be negative even when the average is positive.

Model: Each settlement rate is perturbed by N(0, 80% × |rate|). If the trailing rate is 0.05%, individual settlements might be anywhere from -0.03% to +0.13%.

This reduces effective funding collection versus a model that uses daily averages.

Additional Filters

Beyond basis risk, we apply:

  • Spot availability filter: 23% of symbols (204/874) have no spot pair and are excluded
  • 10% insurance fund deduction: On every profitable close
  • OI cap at 2%: Never exceed 2% of a symbol's open interest

Why We Trust This Number

  1. Conservative by design — every assumption errs toward pessimism
  2. 6.35 years of data — covers bull, bear, and sideways markets (2019-2025)
  3. Out-of-sample validation — last 90 days held out, outperforms train
  4. Basis risk adds realism — most competitors don't model this at all
  5. Spot filter applied — only tradeable symbols included

The 19.55% CAGR is the number we use externally. Under-promise, over-deliver.
