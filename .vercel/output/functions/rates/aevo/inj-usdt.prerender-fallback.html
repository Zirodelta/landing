Aevo INJ/USDT Funding Rate

INJ/USDT perpetual futures on Aevo offer unique funding rate arbitrage opportunities through the platform's hourly funding mechanism. As a DEX built on its own rollup with combined options and perpetuals markets, Aevo provides distinct funding dynamics compared to centralized exchanges.

How Funding Works on Aevo

Aevo calculates funding rates hourly based on the premium between perpetual and mark prices, with payments occurring every hour on the hour. The rollup architecture enables faster settlement while maintaining on-chain transparency for funding calculations. Funding rates are determined by the 8-hour TWAP of the premium, but payments occur more frequently than traditional 8-hour cycles. The off-chain order book provides efficient price discovery while on-chain settlement ensures funding payment integrity.

Arbitrage Opportunities and Execution

Cross-exchange arbitrage between Aevo and centralized exchanges like Binance or OKX can be profitable due to funding rate differentials and timing mismatches. Risk Considerations and Mitigation

Aevo's rollup centralization presents unique counterparty risk compared to fully decentralized protocols, requiring assessment of sequencer reliability and upgrade governance. Smaller liquidity pools mean larger positions face increased slippage risk, particularly during volatile periods when funding arbitrage opportunities are most attractive. The complex product set combining options and perpetuals creates potential for operational errors if traders confuse position types or settlement mechanics. Rollup bridge risks add an additional layer of consideration for capital deployment, though fast withdrawals mitigate some timing risks. Gas costs on the rollup are minimal, but bridge costs must be factored into arbitrage profitability calculations.
