## The Short Answer

Funding rate farming works. The alpha is real - cross-exchange rate spreads routinely hit 20-100%+ annualized on individual pairs. But doing it manually means watching dozens of exchanges, calculating spreads, executing both legs of a hedge within seconds, managing margin across venues, and rotating when rates shift. Funding rates settle every 8 hours. Markets don't sleep. You do.

Zirodelta's Beta-X1 engine automates the entire cycle: scan → identify → execute → monitor → rotate → distribute. Every 15 minutes across 19+ exchanges. 24/7. No human in the loop.

## Why Manual Funding Rate Farming Breaks

### The Math Problem

Funding rates are published across 19+ major exchanges, on thousands of trading pairs, resettling every 8 hours. To find the best cross-exchange spreads, you need to:

1. Pull rates from every exchange simultaneously
2. Match pairs across venues (same asset, different exchange)
3. Compute the delta (difference between venues)
4. Rank by profitability, adjusting for liquidity, margin requirements, and fees
5. Execute both legs of the hedge within seconds
6. Repeat every 8 hours, minimum

That's 19 exchanges × hundreds of pairs × 3 settlements per day = tens of thousands of data points daily. No human can process this in real time.

### The Timing Problem

The best spreads appear and disappear in minutes. A pair showing 80% annualized delta at settlement might compress to 5% within an hour. If you're asleep, at dinner, or just not looking at the right pair - you miss it.

Funding rate farming is a game of coverage: how many pairs can you monitor, how fast can you execute, and how consistently can you capture settlements. Humans cap out at watching 2-3 exchanges and a handful of pairs.

### The Execution Problem

Opening a cross-exchange hedge requires:
1. Sufficient margin on both exchanges
2. Simultaneous entry (or near-simultaneous) to avoid directional exposure
3. Correct sizing to ensure delta neutrality
4. Monitoring for margin health on both legs
5. Knowing when to close - when spreads compress, when funding settles, when better opportunities appear elsewhere

One mistake - one leg filled and the other rejected, one exchange lagging, one margin call you didn't see - and your "risk-free" arbitrage has directional exposure.

### The Fistya Problem

Even expert manual traders hit a ceiling: you can't be awake for every settlement, you can't watch every exchange, and you can't scale beyond what one person can monitor.

Zirodelta exists because the alpha is real but the human bottleneck is real too.

## How Beta-X1 Automates It

### Scan (Every 15 Minutes)

36 exchange adapters pull live funding rates from centralized and decentralized venues:
- **CEX:** Binance, OKX, Gate, Bybit, KuCoin, BitMEX, Deribit, BingX, and more
- **DEX:** Hyperliquid, dYdX, GRVT

The engine groups assets by base currency across exchanges, computes cross-exchange deltas, and ranks by `delta_score` - a composite metric that factors in rate differential, liquidity, historical stability, and margin requirements.

Current coverage: 1.4M+ funding rate records stored in ClickHouse. [Over $150B in daily perpetual futures volume](https://www.bitget.site/academy/perpetual-futures) monitored across venues.

### Identify (Autonomous Decision)

The farming decision engine evaluates:
- Which pairs have the highest risk-adjusted delta
- Available capital across exchanges
- Current open positions (to avoid doubling up or conflicting hedges)
- Exchange-specific constraints (margin requirements, position limits)

Instead of a human scanning for the best rate and rotating between exchanges, the engine scans everything simultaneously and picks the optimal allocation.

### Execute (Hedged-Only)

Architectural rule: **every position is a hedge pair.** Long on Exchange A + short on Exchange B on the same asset. Single-leg exposure is architecturally impossible - the engine won't open one side without the other.

Execution happens through exchange-specific adapters that handle:
- Order type selection (limit vs market based on spread and urgency)
- Slippage management
- Partial fill handling
- Entry timing to minimize brief directional exposure

### Monitor (24/7)

FundingGuard watches all open positions via WebSocket connections:
- Real-time margin health on every leg
- Debounced monitoring to avoid noise-triggered exits
- SmartExit logic: if margin gets thin on one leg, close both legs (never leave a single leg exposed)
- Per-exchange caps prevent over-concentration

### Rotate (Every Epoch)

Every 8 hours, synchronized with funding rate settlements:
- Positions are evaluated against current market rates
- If a pair's spread has compressed, the engine closes it and redeploys to better opportunities
- If a pair is still printing, it stays open
- New deposits activate, withdrawn capital is freed

### Distribute

Revenue from captured spreads is distributed per epoch:
- **60% to vault depositors** (in USDT)
- **25% to protocol growth** (infrastructure, exchange integrations)
- **15% to $ZDLT buyback and burn** (permanent supply reduction)

## What Automation Can't Fix

Being honest about limitations:

- **Exchange risk remains.** Capital lives on exchanges. Automation doesn't prevent exchange insolvency. Diversification across 19+ venues is the mitigation, not elimination.
- **Spread compression in calm markets.** Low volatility means lower rate dispersion. The engine can't create spreads that don't exist. Returns are variable.
- **Smart contract and infrastructure risk.** The engine itself is software. Bugs, downtime, and connectivity issues are real operational risks. (This is why Zirodelta publishes a transparency dashboard - so you can see exactly what the engine is doing.)
- **Collateral reflexivity.** $ZDLT price affects collateral value, which affects deployed capital, which affects revenue. The auto-deleverage system protects depositors, but the relationship exists.
