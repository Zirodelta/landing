## What Are Funding Rates?

Funding rates are payments between traders to keep perpetual futures aligned with spot prices. Every 1-8 hours (depending on the exchange), one side pays the other.

**When funding is positive:** Longs pay shorts (bullish sentiment)
**When funding is negative:** Shorts pay longs (bearish sentiment)

The rates are typically small (0.01% to 0.1% per settlement), but during volatile periods, they can spike to extreme levels: 0.3%, 0.5%, even 1%+ per settlement. On an 8-hour cycle, 0.3% per settlement equals 32.8% annualized.

**Example:** BTC/USDT funding at 0.4% on Binance means longs pay 0.4% of their position value to shorts. If you're short 10 BTC worth $500K, you receive $2,000 in 8 hours. No directional exposure if properly hedged.

## Why Extreme Rates Are Free Money

When funding rates hit ≥0.3%, the math becomes overwhelmingly favorable for delta-neutral arbitrage:

### The Strategy
1. **Buy spot** (go long the underlying asset)
2. **Short perp** (equal position size on perpetual futures)
3. **Collect funding** when longs pay shorts
4. **Close both positions** after settlement

**Net exposure:** Zero. Price goes up? Spot gains offset perp losses. Price goes down? Perp gains offset spot losses. You're purely capturing the funding rate, not betting on direction.
