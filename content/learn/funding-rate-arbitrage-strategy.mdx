## The Strategy in One Paragraph

**Funding rate arbitrage captures income from perpetual futures funding payments while maintaining zero directional exposure to price movements.** Execute by simultaneously buying spot and shorting an equal amount of perpetual futures. When funding is positive, shorts receive payments from longs. Your spot position hedges price risk from the short perpetual, leaving pure funding rate yield. Cross-exchange execution amplifies this by capturing rate differentials between venues.

Our backtest over 6.35 years shows 19.55% CAGR with 3.74 Sharpe ratio and maximum drawdown of just 3.48%. Real money validation: Zirodelta's Beta-X1 engine achieved 11.8% return in 12 days of live trading.

## How Delta Neutral Hedging Works

**The core principle:** For every $1 of spot you buy, short $1 of perpetual futures. Price movements cancel out, leaving funding payments as pure profit.

**Example with $10,000 capital:**
- Buy $10,000 worth of BTC spot at $71,250
- Short $10,000 worth of BTC perpetual at $71,312
- Net exposure: Zero (long spot cancels short perp)

**Price moves up to $75,000:**
- Spot position gains: +$528 
- Perpetual position loses: -$528
- Net P&L from price: $0

**Price moves down to $67,500:**
- Spot position loses: -$528
- Perpetual position gains: +$528  
- Net P&L from price: $0

**Funding settlement (8 hours later):**
- Funding rate: +0.087% (longs pay shorts)
- Funding received: $10,000 × 0.087% = $8.70
- Annualized yield: 0.087% × 3 × 365 = 95.2%

The beauty is mathematical: regardless of whether Bitcoin goes to $100,000 or $40,000, your P&L comes purely from funding payments, not price direction.

## Step-by-Step Execution Flow

### Phase 1: Market Analysis

**1. Scan funding rate opportunities**
Use [Zirodelta's rate scanner](/rates) or build your own monitoring system. Look for:
- Sustained positive funding (>1% annualized minimum)
- Sufficient liquidity in both spot and perpetual markets
- Low spread between bid/ask on both instruments

**2. Calculate net yield after costs**
```
Net Yield = (Funding Rate × Frequency) - (Spot Trading Fees + Perp Trading Fees + Funding Fees)
```

For Binance example:
- Funding rate: +0.087% per 8h
- Spot trading fees: 0.075% (maker) to 0.10% (taker)  
- Perp trading fees: 0.02% (maker) to 0.04% (taker)
- Net yield: ~0.08% per 8h after costs = 87% annualized

**3. Position sizing**
Conservative approach: Never exceed 20% of total capital in a single pair. Diversify across 5-10 pairs to reduce single-pair risk.

### Phase 2: Entry Execution

**4. Fund exchanges**
- Transfer capital to both spot and perpetual trading venues
- For cross-exchange arbitrage, split capital between different exchanges
- Maintain 2-3x the position size in available margin (conservative leverage)

**5. Simultaneous execution**
Critical: Both legs must execute as close to simultaneously as possible to minimize directional exposure.

**Same-exchange execution:**
1. Place spot buy order (limit order at current ask)
2. Immediately place perpetual short order (same notional size)
3. Monitor fills - adjust if only one leg fills

**Cross-exchange execution:**
1. Execute spot buy on Exchange A
2. Simultaneously execute perpetual short on Exchange B  
3. Higher complexity but captures cross-exchange rate differential

**6. Position verification**
Confirm delta neutrality:
```
Delta = (Spot Position Value) + (Perp Position Value) ≈ 0
```

Small deltas (<5% of position size) are acceptable due to execution timing and spread differences.

### Phase 3: Management and Exit

**7. Funding collection**
- Funding settles automatically at scheduled intervals
- Monitor funding rate changes - exit if rates turn consistently negative
- Rebalance if positions drift due to price movements

**8. Exit triggers**
- **Profit target:** Close when accumulated funding reaches target (e.g., 2-5% of capital)
- **Rate decay:** Close when funding drops below break-even after fees
- **Market stress:** Close during extreme volatility when delta hedging becomes difficult
- **Opportunity cost:** Close to redeploy capital to higher-yielding pairs

**9. Systematic exit**
1. Close perpetual short position first (faster execution)
2. Immediately close spot position
3. Calculate realized P&L including all fees and funding payments

## Capital Requirements and Position Sizing

**Minimum viable capital:** $5,000-$10,000 per exchange to overcome fixed costs and minimum position sizes. Below this, trading fees consume too much of funding yield.

**Recommended allocation:**
- **Conservative:** 10% of portfolio, max 5% per pair
- **Moderate:** 20% of portfolio, max 8% per pair  
- **Aggressive:** 30%+ of portfolio, max 15% per pair

**Cross-exchange capital split:**
For cross-exchange arbitrage, maintain 50-70% of capital on the exchange where you're short perpetuals (margin requirements) and 30-50% where you're long spot.

**Leverage considerations:**
- **Spot:** 1x leverage (no margin needed for long spot positions)
- **Perpetuals:** 2-5x leverage maximum (conservative approach)
- **Total portfolio leverage:** Should not exceed 2-3x to prevent liquidation risk

Higher leverage amplifies yield but increases liquidation risk. Zirodelta's engine operates at 5x with auto-deleveraging when positions approach risk thresholds.

## Cross-Exchange Routing Advantage

**Why cross-exchange arbitrage beats same-exchange:**

**1. Rate dispersion:** Different exchanges have different trader populations and rate calculation methods. Rate differentials of 10-50% between exchanges are common.

**2. Timing arbitrage:** Exchanges settle funding at different intervals. When Hyperliquid settles hourly and Binance settles every 8 hours, significant rate divergence can persist.

**3. Liquidity fragmentation:** Whales impact funding rates differently across venues. A large long position on Bybit might not affect Binance rates immediately.

**Real example (March 13, 2026):**
- **MATIC/USDT on KuCoin:** +0.15% per 4h (328% annualized)
- **MATIC/USDT on Binance:** +0.03% per 8h (32% annualized)  
- **Cross-exchange spread:** 296% annualized

Execute: Long MATIC perpetual on KuCoin (+328% funding), Short MATIC perpetual on Binance (-32% funding), capture 296% spread regardless of MATIC price direction.

- **Rate decay:** Funding drops below 3% annualized for 24+ hours
- **Profitability target:** Accumulated 3-8% total return from funding payments

- **Better opportunity:** Higher-yielding pair becomes available (opportunity cost)

**Dynamic rebalancing:**
Monitor every funding settlement. If a pair's rate drops while another pair's rate increases, close the first and redeploy to the second. Active management significantly improves overall returns.

## Common Execution Mistakes

**1. Neglecting fees:** Trading fees often exceed 0.2% round-trip. Factor all costs into yield calculations.

**2. Imperfect hedging:** Small deltas compound over time. Rebalance when delta exceeds 3% of position size.

**3. Emotional exits:** Closing positions during temporary rate reversals instead of waiting for trend confirmation.

**4. Over-leveraging:** Using high leverage to amplify returns increases liquidation risk dramatically.

**5. Ignoring settlement timing:** Not accounting for when funding actually settles can create timing mismatches.

**6. Single-exchange focus:** Missing higher-yield cross-exchange opportunities by staying on one venue.
