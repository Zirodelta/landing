## The Strategy

When a funding rate spikes to extreme levels (≥0.3% per settlement period), enter a delta-neutral position: **buy spot + short perpetual**. Collect one funding payment at settlement. Close 30 minutes after.

No overnight risk. No multi-day holds. One funding event, one collection, done.

This is the opposite of Beta-X1's persistent position strategy (hold for days/weeks at moderate rates). The sniper targets short-lived extremes for quick extraction.

## The Critical Question: Do Extreme Rates Persist?

Our [margin mode research](/research/margin-mode-dead) proved that extreme **negative** funding rates are transient spikes — they mean-revert before you can profit. Does the same apply to extreme positive rates?

**No. Extreme positive rates persist.**

| What happens after an extreme settlement | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Still extreme (≥0.2%) at next settlement | **39%** |
| Still elevated (≥0.1%) at next settlement | **64.7%** |
| Collapsed to normal (&lt;0.01%) | 17.1% |
| Average rate retention | 51.9% of original |

This is structurally different from negative rates. Extreme positive funding is driven by **leverage demand** — traders piling into long positions on trending tokens. This demand persists across multiple settlement periods. Negative rates (short squeezes) resolve within one settlement.

**Key insight:** The sniper collects the extreme rate at settlement T. Even if the rate drops at T+1, you're already closed. The persistence data tells us entry timing is reliable — the rate you see is the rate you collect.

## Risk Factors

This backtest has limitations. The following costs are **not** included in the numbers above:

1. **Execution slippage.** Order book impact when entering/exiting. Small positions ($50—200) on liquid pairs have minimal slippage. Illiquid micro-cap alts could eat the entire edge.

2. **Price drift during hold.** The position is held for ~1 hour (entry → settlement → 30min close delay). Delta-neutral hedging minimizes but doesn't eliminate basis movement. Estimated 0.05—0.1% adverse drift on volatile tokens.

3. **Spot-perp spread at entry.** The basis between spot and perp varies. Entering when the spread is unfavorable adds hidden cost.

4. **Funding rate prediction error.** The displayed "next funding rate" and the actually settled rate can differ slightly on some exchanges.

## Why ≥0.3% Is the Right Threshold

The 0.2—0.3% bucket contains 10,121 settlements (65% of all extremes) but nets only +0.039% per snipe after fees. Any real-world friction — slippage, drift, spread — can turn this bucket negative.

At ≥0.3%:
- 5,368 settlements over 6 years (2.4/day average)
- +0.279% net per snipe (7x the 0.2% bucket)
- 100% win rate after 0.2% RT costs
- Survives 0.1% adverse drift with room to spare

**We set Zidee's minimum threshold at 0.3%.**
