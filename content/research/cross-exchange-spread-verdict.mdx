## The Hypothesis

Funding rates on the same symbol differ across exchanges. If Binance BTC funding is +0.01% and Bybit is +0.03%, go long Binance perp (pay less) and short Bybit perp (collect more). When rates converge, close both.

The spread between exchange rates should mean-revert - it's driven by the same underlying market.

## The Analysis

We ran a full cointegration analysis on 15 major symbols across Binance, Bybit, KuCoin, and Hyperliquid. Two time windows: in-sample (Nov 2025 – Feb 2026) and out-of-sample (May – Aug 2025).

### Mean-Reversion Is Real

All 15 symbols confirm mean-reversion in both time windows:
- Autocorrelation(1) positive for 15/15 symbols in both IS and OOS
- Half-lives: 1-9 hours, stable across regimes
- This is perhaps a physical law - arbitrageurs force convergence

### But the Edge Is Too Small

| Cost Scenario | In-Sample Profitable | Out-of-Sample Profitable |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| 0 bps | 100% (45/45 pairs) | 100% (15/15 pairs) |
| 1 bps RT | 24% (11/45) | **0% (0/15)** |
| 2 bps RT | 2% (1/45) | 0% (0/15) |
| 3 bps RT | 0% (0/45) | 0% (0/15) |

Average raw edge: **+0.85 bps/trade** in-sample, **+0.60 bps/trade** out-of-sample.

The only pair that barely survives 2 bps cost: OP Binance↔OKX (+0.36 bps/trade, 57 trades over 90 days). Not enough to build a strategy on.

## Why It Fails

1. **Funding rates settle infrequently** (every 1-8 hours), but you need to hold the position through settlement to collect. This limits trade frequency.

2. **Both legs are perpetual futures**, so you pay fees twice - once on each exchange. Minimum realistic RT cost is ~2 bps with maker orders on both sides.

3. **The spread is small by nature** - exchanges compete on rates, keeping them close. The very mechanism that creates mean-reversion (arbitrageurs) also compresses the edge.

## What This Means

The mean-reversion signal is useful as a **timing indicator** (when to enter/exit positions), but not as a standalone profit source. We use it in v3 for rotation decisions - switching between exchanges when a better rate appears.

The core profit comes from the **absolute** funding rate (spot-vs-perp), not the **spread** between exchange rates.

## Conclusion

**Strategy status: DEAD.**

Not revisitable without a fundamental change in fee structure (sub-0.5 bps maker on both legs) or a shift in market microstructure that widens rate differentials beyond 2 bps consistently.
