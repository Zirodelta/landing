## The Journey

We didn't start with spot-vs-perp. We started with cross-exchange interval arbitrage in early 2025 - the idea that settlement frequency differences between exchanges (Binance 8h vs Hyperliquid 1h) could be exploited for profit.

It couldn't. Not after fees.

This document traces the full path from v1 to v3, every dead end included.

## v1: Interval Arbitrage

**The idea:** Collect funding on high-frequency exchanges (Hyperliquid 1h, KuCoin 4h) while hedging on low-frequency exchanges (Binance 8h). The extra settlements = edge.

**The result:** ~2.5% CAGR with an 89% cost ratio. Fees ate nearly everything. The frequency differential exists but the spread per settlement is microscopic - fractions of a basis point. Hundreds of trades needed, each costing 30bps round-trip.

**Verdict:** Dead. No parameter tuning saves it.

## v2: Persistence Scoring

**The idea:** Score symbols by how persistently they maintain positive funding rates over 365 days. Allocate fixed $20K positions to the top scorers. Monthly rotation.

**The result:** ~11.4% CAGR. Decent, but most capital sat idle. Fixed $20K positions on a $300K account meant only 4-5 positions deployed at any time. Monthly rotation was too slow to capture short-lived opportunities.

**What we learned:** The scoring logic was correct. The execution was wrong.

## Cross-Exchange Spread Arbitrage

**The idea:** Mean-reversion trading on funding rate spreads between exchanges. When Binance rate diverges from Bybit rate, trade the convergence.

We ran a full cointegration analysis across 15 symbols with in-sample and out-of-sample validation.

**The result:**

| Cost | In-Sample Profitable | Out-of-Sample Profitable |
|------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| 0 bps | 100% | 100% |
| 1 bps | 24% | 0% |
| 2 bps | 2% | 0% |

The edge is real (~0.6-0.85 bps/trade) but smaller than the cost to capture it. Published separately: [Cross-Exchange Spread Arb: The Verdict](/research/cross-exchange-spread-verdict).

## Margin Mode (Negative Funding)

**The idea:** Short spot (borrow + sell) and long perp to capture negative funding rates. This would let the engine earn in bear markets too.

We collected borrow rates from Bybit (231 coins) and Binance (435 coins), cross-referenced against funding rates, and ran a backtest.

**The result:** 4 trades, ALL lost money. 0% win rate. -$2,648 over the test period.

**Root cause:** Negative funding rates are transient spikes that mean-revert before borrow costs are covered. Positive rates are structurally persistent (overleveraged longs). You can't flip the strategy. Published separately: [Margin Mode: Dead on Arrival](/research/margin-mode-dead).

## v3: Spot-vs-Perp Dynamic Allocation

Three changes from v2 that compounded into a 2× improvement:

### 1. Dynamic Position Sizing

Instead of fixed $20K:

```
size = min(
    2% of open interest,
    20% of equity,
    20% of equity per symbol,
    remaining capital
)
```

Positions scale with equity (compounding). Capital utilization approaches 100%. This single change nearly doubled CAGR.

### 2. Spot-vs-Perp Structure

v2 used perp-vs-perp (long perp + short perp across exchanges) = double perp fees.
v3 uses spot-vs-perp (buy spot + short perp on same exchange) = cheaper, captures absolute rate.

### 3. 1-Day Lookback

v2 used 365-day lookback for stability. v3 uses 1-day - maximum reactivity. We tested 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, and 365 days. 1-day was optimal.
